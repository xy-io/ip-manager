Concept A
Two-zone header

Left zone owns identity — the app name and the active network. Right zone owns actions, but reduced to only what you actually reach for regularly. Everything that's occasional (Proxmox, ARP Scan, DNS) collapses into one Tools menu. The status badge and view controls move out of the main row into their own quieter strip.

What moved into Tools ▾ — Proxmox, ARP Scan, Ping, DNS, CIDR Calculator, QR Codes, Subnet Visualiser. The dropdown gains a short section header ("Network ops", "Utilities") to keep it scannable as it grows. Help also moves here or into Settings.
Concept B
Progressive disclosure by role

The toolbar has two modes. Everyday mode shows only what you use constantly — Ping, Import, Export, view toggle. Admin mode is toggled on in Settings (or via a small toggle in the toolbar) and reveals the power tools. The idea is that a user who just wants to look up an IP shouldn't share a toolbar with a user who's auditing their entire subnet.

Practical detail — The toggle would live in Settings → Display as "Show advanced toolbar" with a one-line explanation ("Displays Proxmox, ARP Scan, DNS and network management tools in the toolbar"). Defaults to off for new installs. This also makes the mobile view much less chaotic by default, since the collapsing Tools menu only needs to contain the 3 everyday items.
Bonus idea — hybrid of both

The two approaches aren't mutually exclusive. The two-zone layout works at any density level, and progressive disclosure controls what populates the right zone. Together they'd give you a clean default and a power-user mode that still feels structured.
